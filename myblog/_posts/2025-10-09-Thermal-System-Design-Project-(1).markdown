In the 2024 Autumn Semester, I had a project about choosing a future refrigerant and designing the thermal cycle of a refrigerator. The project's goal was selecting a future refrigerant while considering ODP(Ozone Depletion Potential) and GWP(Global Warming Potential). As you know, [R-134a](https://en.wikinpedia.org/wiki/1,1,1,2-Tetrafluoroethane) is the general refrigerant, but there are some reasons *Not to Use It*.

## Why Did we Use the Refrigerant *R-134a*?

From the 1930s to the middle of 1990s, general household refrigerator used to employ chlorofluorocarbon (CFC) Refrigerant,[R-12](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dichlorodifluoromethane). But at the end of the 20's century, ozone depletion was on the rise and R-12 was phased out globally under the [Montreal Protocol](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Montreal_Protocol) because it had a high ODP (1.0) among refrigerants.

![R-12's ODP](./assets/img/posts/20251009/12odp.png)
<small>Refrigerants ODP. image by [EnggCyclopedia](https://enggcyclopedia.com/2012/01/types-refrigerants/)</small>

So, newer refrigerants with zero ODP, such as hydrofluorocarbon (HFC) refrigerants like R-134a, were introduced and rapidly replaced R-12 in refrigerators and air conditioning (AC) systems.

## The Fall of R-134a

However, R-134a's dominance was short-lived. Global warming is the hottest issue nowadays. Sadly, R-134a contributes significantly to global warming.

![R-134a's GWP](./assets/img/posts/20251009/gwp.jpg)
<small>Refrigerants GWP. image by [UNILAB](https://www.unilab.eu/articles/technical-articles/thermodynamic-engineering-articles/new-frontiers-synthetic-refrigerants-low-gwp-fluids/)</small>

R-134a has about 1430 GWP, which means 1430 times more potent at warming the planet than the same amount of carbon dioxide over 100 years. This is part of the broader effort to find environmentally friendly refrigerants. [According to the EPA (United States Environmental Protection Agency), the refrigerant, HFC-134a, will no longer be allowed in nonroad vehicles as of January 1, 2028.](https://www.epa.gov/mvac/acceptable-refrigerants-and-their-impacts) 

![F-gas Regualtion](./assets/img/posts/20251009/eufregulation.jpg)
<small>F-gas Regulation. image by [Coolingstyle](https://coolingstyle.com/the-end-of-r134a-in-europe-future-bans-and-the-green-transformation-of-the-refrigeration-industry/)</small>

[EU's F-gas Regulation also banned the use of HFC-134a in new equipment.Under the regulation, new commercial equipment can't use R-134a from 2022, with full phase-out expected by 2025.](https://coolingstyle.com/the-end-of-r134a-in-europe-future-bans-and-the-green-transformation-of-the-refrigeration-industry/) Plus, Earlier bans apply to domestic refrigeration (from 2015) and mobile AC (from around 2017).

That's why we are stopping the use of R-134a and selecting a future refrigerant. Next time, I'll discuss choosing a future refrigerant using heat engineering principles.
